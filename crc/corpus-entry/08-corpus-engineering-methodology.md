Corpus engineering uses a spec-then-apply discipline. Planning artifacts remain private until Faheem promotes them into the public surface.

## Working Method

Read live files first. Draft a spec. Route decisions. Apply only after authorization. Edit by text anchor and re-read each anchor against the live file immediately before editing. Apply one edit at a time. If an anchor does not match, stop and report.

## Publication Discipline

Do not stage, commit, or push without explicit authorization. Avoid broad staging. Show the intended staged set and verify it.

## Writing Discipline

Public formal writing should state the problem first, avoid em dashes, avoid decorative metaphors, and distinguish evidence, doctrine, navigation, and process.

## Boundary Discipline

Private files such as `state.md`, `system-prompt.md`, workflow prompts, and pre-ratification specs are not public corpus doctrine unless Faheem explicitly promotes them.
