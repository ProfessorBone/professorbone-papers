# Constitutional Coherence: Baseline Generations, Cross-Surface Consistency, and the Governance of Multi-Surface Drift

*This companion paper is currently in development.*

Constitutional Baselines v1.2 governs baseline authority one surface at a time:
who may establish, pin, amend, version, and retire the standard against which a
single monitored surface is measured. It closes the per-surface baseline-authority
dependency, but it scaffolds rather than completes the cross-surface question. The
corpus now has several baselines on several surfaces (verdict, store, construction,
salience, compression, view, and the non-memory Observe baselines still to come),
and each can be individually doctrine-grounded and current while the set is mutually
incoherent.

This companion will govern the coherence of the baseline family. It will mature the
BaselineGeneration object that Constitutional Baselines scaffolds: what counts as one
generation, who authorizes it, how a single-surface amendment interacts with the
generation it belongs to, whether partial generation updates are admissible, how
cross-surface coherence is checked, and how generation-level reconstitution differs
from surface-level re-anchoring. It will formalize Baseline Family Incoherence, the
failure in which individually valid baselines diverge as a family (for example a
verdict baseline that grows stricter while a construction baseline grows narrower, so
the construction surface hides the very ambiguity the verdict baseline expects to
escalate), as distinct from P_base4 version skew.

It will also take up the two adjacent human-interface governance residues that
Constitutional Baselines named but did not govern: the threshold-authority problem
(the baseline / metric / threshold / trigger distinction, and threshold capture, in
which a doctrine-grounded baseline is blinded by a quietly raised threshold), and the
evidence-packet provenance problem behind mediated capture (whether the briefing
through which a sovereign re-anchors traces to the monitored process). Whether these
belong in this companion or in a dedicated threshold-governance companion is itself an
open question this paper will settle.

Series position: Companion 4 to Constitutional Runtime Computation v5.3, continuing the
memory, retrieval, and baselines companions. Seeded by the cross-baseline coherence and
Baseline Family Incoherence open problems, the scaffolded BaselineGeneration object, and
the threshold-authority and evidence-packet-provenance residues named in Constitutional
Baselines v1.2.
