# Constitutional Thresholds: Trigger Authority, Decision Boundaries, and Evidence-Packet Provenance in Governed Drift Monitoring

*This companion paper is currently in development.*

The corpus now governs the substrate (the core paper), memory operations
(Companion 1), memory-derived Observe construction (Companion 2), each measurement
standard surface by surface (Companion 3), and the coherence of the standard-set as
a family (Companion 4). Across that arc, two adjacent human-interface governance
residues have been named and deliberately left out of scope, most recently on the
axis argument of Constitutional Coherence v1.2: the threshold-authority problem and
the evidence-packet-provenance problem behind mediated capture. This companion will
govern both.

The threshold-authority problem is the vertical residue. A baseline can stay
sovereign-anchored and a metric can measure honestly while the decision boundary at
which a measured deviation triggers action is quietly raised until real drift no
longer crosses it. This is capture of the decision boundary rather than of the
standard. The companion will draw the baseline, metric, threshold, and trigger
distinction precisely, establish who may set or change a threshold, how thresholds
are versioned, how an authorized sensitivity adjustment is distinguished from
threshold drift, and how the four objects coordinate so that a doctrine-grounded
baseline cannot be blinded by a threshold the monitored process can move.

The evidence-packet-provenance problem is the residue that Constitutional Coherence
v1.2 elevated from an open problem to a deployment precondition. The more the
architecture routes terminal judgments to the sovereign, the more the integrity of
the sovereign's evidence path becomes part of the constitutional substrate. A
sovereign who re-anchors a baseline, or re-coheres a generation, on a briefing
generated from the monitored surfaces has been captured one step removed, through
the human interface, and no structural conjunct catches it. The companion will
govern whether the evidence packet through which the sovereign decides traces to the
monitored process, so that the sovereign-facing briefing is itself a governed object
rather than an ungoverned channel into the terminal authority.

The two problems meet at the seam Constitutional Coherence v1.2 flagged but did not
govern: the cross-surface threshold, a trigger condition defined over more than one
surface, or a threshold whose authorized level on one surface depends on the standard
set on another. Once the BaselineGeneration exists, cross-surface thresholds are not
an edge case. The companion will likely need to govern threshold families or threshold
generations, not only single-surface thresholds, which is the point at which threshold
authority and the coherence of the standard-set become one question.
